DEFENCE MECHANISMS

Methods to protect oneself and others from cybercriminals and malware.

Prevention techniques

Be wary of fake links or attachments - do not open any suspicious attachments, links or forward the email to another device because they may contain malware

Do not provide any personal or financial information to unverified sources even to someone familiar. If the email was sent by somebody familiar but it seems out of character or suspicious, contact the person by phone number or physically to confirm whether they had actually sent it

Ensure to check settings regularly - by making sure that the system is up to date, it can protect itself from contracting malware

Ensuring pop up blocker is on - this reduces the likelihood of falling for scareware

Do not store login credentials on devices or word documents, it makes them easily accessible for anyone who manages to gain access to the device

Complete a virus scan prior to opening any new programs or files that contain executable code

Make it a habit to back up data regularly, so in the event of a virus infection, valuable data files are not lost

Viewing images in plain text - Although most malware are only activiated when the user opens the attachment or link, however if the victim's email client allows scripting it is possible to obtain a virus by opening the message. To protect the computer system, it is best to limit what HTML is avaialble in the email messages

Install an antivirus software, make sure to update it and use it regularly to protect the user's system from contracting malware

What to if someone has fallen victim to a phishing scam

If someone has fallen for a phishing scam, it is advised to disconnect the device from the internet immediately, as it will reduce the risk of the malware spreading to other devices on the network.

After disconnecting from the internet, start backing up files in external hard drives or a cloud storage service such as DropBox or Google Drive, this in case the data gets erased during the recovery process of the phishing attack.

Change login credentials immediately, if the victim has made login attempts into a fake website, go into the real website and change the account's name and password immediately. If they have tried to access your account through a fake website, the attackers will use the login information provided to access the victim's account.
